My Projects & Research

Analysis Of Different Image Processing Techniques For Crack Detection In Concrete Pipes

Published in the International Journal of Researches in Biosciences, Agriculture and Technology, this paper addresses the critical issue of automated crack detection in concrete pipes, essential for assessing pipe deterioration. Manual inspection is impractical for large-scale or underground pipes due to its time-consuming and subjective nature. We evaluated three image processing techniques—Morphological Approach, Gaussian and Prewitt Filter, and Sobel Filter—by assessing their accuracy, precision, and execution time to determine the most efficient method for crack detection. The process involved collecting images, removing noise, converting them to grayscale or binary forms, and applying edge detection and segmentation techniques. Our findings demonstrated that automated image analysis methods are effective and efficient alternatives to manual inspection, providing reliable crack detection and measurement.

Anemia Level Detection

This project focuses on accurately predicting anemia levels using six advanced machine learning algorithms: K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Naive Bayes, Logistic Regression, Support Vector Machines (SVM), Decision Trees, and Gradient Boosting. Among these, Gradient Boosting stood out with an accuracy of 93.56%, excelling in predicting anemia levels across diverse scenarios. We identified 16 key features crucial for precise predictive modeling, encompassing demographic, health, and lifestyle factors. The application interface, built with Streamlit—a Python library—provides a user-friendly platform. Users can input relevant data to receive immediate predictions about their anemia level, facilitating accessible and actionable health insights.

This project implements a content-based movie recommendation system in Python, using cosine similarity to suggest the top 5 movies based on user input. The system leverages tools like Jupyter for interactive development, NLTK for natural language processing tasks, and PyCharm for efficient coding. American Bankruptcy Prediction

The U.S. Company Bankruptcy Prediction Dataset aims to predict financial distress or bankruptcy among companies, crucial for stakeholders such as investors, creditors, policymakers, and business analysts. By employing ensemble learning techniques like Random Forest and XGBoost, the project achieves high accuracy in identifying companies at risk of bankruptcy, especially effective given the dataset's imbalance where 94% of entries are labeled as 'alive'. The analysis reveals Random Forest's precision of 0.79 for predicting 'failed' status, highlighting its superiority over K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) in handling such predictive tasks. This project supports informed decision-making in risk management, investment strategies, and policy formulation within the financial sector.
